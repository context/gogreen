Media

Is there really global warming pollution on the information superhighway? Certainly, the flow of words, ideas, and entertainment seems clean enough. But major media companies are some of the largest corporations in the world -- with employees across the globe, countless suppliers and distributors, and a stronghold on the hearts and minds of people like you. And some media companies, while perhaps best known for their role in worldwide communications, are large conglomerates that have their hands in scores of different businesses. The Media sector is particularly important in the role it should play in providing useful, substantive information on issues like climate change to audiences that are ready for it.

The higher the score, the greater the company's commitment to fighting global warming

Raise Your Voice uses a 0-to-100 point scale and 22 criteria to determine if companies have:


  • MEASURED their climate "footprint"
  • REDUCED their impact on global warming
  • SUPPORTED (or suggest intent to block) progressive climate legislation
  • Publicly DISCLOSED their climate actions clearly and comprehensively

GreenOrder, a leading sustainability strategy firm, provided strategic guidance on the Raise Your Voice program, assisted in the development of the scoring system, and verified the scoring results for accuracy.

  • Of course, please support high-ranked companies when you shop and invest.
